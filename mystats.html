My favorite food is Dosa

Dosa is a thin, crispy Indian food loved by many. It's simple, made with basic ingredients, and has a tasty, slightly bitter flavor. You can enjoy it as a snack, for breakfast, or whenever you want a yummy, savory meal!


let's explore the different types of dosa batter.
Regular dosa — Made up of a simple base of rice and lentil flour.
Neer dosa — The word neer literally means water in the Tulu language. Neer dosa batter is made from rice flour from Mangalore; the texture is watery and not thick like a typical dosa batter.
Rava dosa —Made up of Rava (semolina), rice flour, and all-purpose flour (maida).
Pesarattu dosa — Made from a mixture of moong dal flour and rice flour.


There are also versions of dosa distinguished by their different fillings:
Masala dosa — Made up of rice, lentils, potato, methi (fenugreek), and curry leaves. Served with chutneys and sambar.
Cheese dosa — Cheese dosa is typically prepared with fermented lentils, rice, and cheese.
